DXY Pivot Watch: Five Data Points That Matter

Why this matters. USD trend changes tend to cascade across gold, crypto, and commodities. A simple, consistent data set keeps you ahead of inflection points.

1) Real yield spreads. Track US 10y TIPS minus Germany and minus Japan. If both spreads compress for three weeks, USD upside momentum often fades.

2) Oil and terms of trade. Rising oil with flat USD often precedes a dollar pullback because petro-importers recycle fewer dollars. Combine Brent trends with DXY to gauge pressure.

4) Options skew. 25-delta risk reversals in EURUSD and USDJPY show where hedges sit. Persistent EURUSD call bid (skew > 0) hints at upside in the pair and downside in DXY.

5) Positioning and carry. CFTC USD net longs plus 3m implied/realized vol. If positioning is crowded long USD while vol is cheap, a squeeze lower is likely.
