Why stablecoins matter. USDT/USDC supply acts like dry powder. Rising float usually means more spot bids and higher BTC support levels. Shrinking float signals less capacity to absorb dips.

Three signals to track.

  • Net issuance: 7-day change in total USDT+USDC. Rising by >1% per week is bullish; falling for three weeks warns of weaker floors.
  • Exchange reserves: If stablecoin reserves on exchanges rise while BTC reserves fall, buyers are staging; the opposite hints at impending sell pressure.
  • Velocity: On-chain transfer volume divided by supply. Low velocity with rising supply = patient bidders; high velocity with flat supply = churny, less supportive.

How to use the data.

  • When net issuance and reserves both climb, raise spot allocation modestly and reduce leverage.
  • If issuance stalls but funding spikes positive, expect squeezes; fade leverage, keep tight stops.
  • Pair trades: long BTC vs. short alt basket during issuance growth; invert when issuance contracts and alt funding is hot.

Invalidation. A sharp rise in real yields or a regulatory shock to stablecoin issuers can break these relationships. Keep a hard weekly close stop below the 200-day moving average.
