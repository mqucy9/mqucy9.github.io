Why use gold? Gold is not a perfect inflation hedge every month, but over multi-year cycles it preserves purchasing power and diversifies equity and bond risk. The key is to size positions and rebalance with discipline instead of guessing CPI prints.

Pairing assets. Gold works best when paired with instruments that also react to real rates. Common pairs: TIPS, broad commodities, or energy equities. Keep the pair small (half the gold notional) to avoid overexposure to one macro factor.

Rebalancing cadence. Rebalance monthly or when gold drifts 2% of portfolio weight away from target. During inflation spikes, rebalance more often to harvest volatility instead of letting the weight balloon.

Execution checklist.

  • Position size = 1R / ATR(14). Cap R at 1% of equity.
  • Enter on pullbacks to 50-day moving average while 10y TIPS yield is falling.
  • Trim 25% of the position after a 10% move in 30 trading days; reload on a 5% dip if TIPS continue lower.
  • Stop-loss: weekly close below 200-day moving average or when real yields rise 50 bps in a month.

When it fails. Gold can lag when real yields jump or the dollar rallies sharply. Avoid adding during rapid hikes in policy rates; wait for the pace of hikes to slow or for real yields to flatten.
